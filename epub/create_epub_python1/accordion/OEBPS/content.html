Accordion

This thesis presents different techniques for achieving different kinds of anonymity in computer networks. In the first part of the thesis, the techniques are described and their anonymity properties are analyzed. Also, in the first part a survey of various existing peer-to-peer, anonymizing systems is presented. In the second part of the thesis, a design and a prototype implementation of an anonymous, low-latency, peer-to-peer file storage system, Accordion, built on practical and efficient DC-networks is presented and its anonymity properties analyzed. The system offers sender anonymity for publishers of files and for the nodes in the network that store the files. The prototype is tested in different ways to increase confidence in the design.
